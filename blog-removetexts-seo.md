## The Technology Behind AI Text Removal

RemoveTexts uses deep learning inpainting technology to reconstruct backgrounds after text removal. The process works in three stages:

1. **Text Detection**: A computer vision model identifies all text regions in the image, mapping their exact boundaries at the pixel level.

2. **Mask Generation**: The detected text areas are converted into a mask that tells the inpainting model exactly which pixels need to be reconstructed.

3. **Background Reconstruction**: The inpainting neural network analyzes the surrounding context -- textures, colors, patterns, lighting -- and generates new pixels that blend seamlessly with the existing image.

This three-stage pipeline is why AI text removal produces results that look natural rather than smeared or blurred. The AI does not just delete pixels; it understands what should be there and recreates it.

## Tips for Getting the Best Results

While AI text removal is remarkably effective, a few practices will help you get the cleanest output:

- **Use high-resolution source images.** The more pixel data the AI has to work with around the text, the better the reconstruction.
- **Choose the right mode.** If you only want some text removed, use "Remove Specific Text" rather than "Remove All Text" to avoid accidentally erasing text you want to keep.
- **Check complex backgrounds.** Solid or simple backgrounds reconstruct almost perfectly. Highly detailed areas behind text (faces, intricate patterns) may need a second pass.
- **Supported formats matter.** RemoveTexts accepts JPG, PNG, and WebP images up to 10 MB. For best quality, use PNG when available.
